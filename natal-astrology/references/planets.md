# Planets — archetype + by-domain keywords

Modern-psychological framing. These are raw material for synthesis, NOT lines to recite. A reading
must always combine a planet's archetype with its sign, house, and aspects in THIS chart.

For each body: **Core archetype**, then keyword hooks per life domain
(career · love · money · family · health · growth).

## Mars
- **Core archetype:** drive, assertion, desire, how you pursue and fight; the will.
- **career:** initiative, competition, leadership under pressure, tolerance for conflict, pace.
- **love:** attraction style, sexual energy, pursuit vs. patience, how anger shows up with partners.
- **money:** risk appetite, earning through effort/enterprise, impulsive vs. strategic spending.
- **family:** how you handle confrontation at home, protectiveness, friction with authority figures.
- **health:** energy/stamina, inflammation/heat themes, accident-proneness when rushed, exercise need.
- **growth:** reclaiming healthy anger, courage, acting on desire without burning bridges.

## Sun
- **Core archetype:** identity, vitality, conscious purpose, where you shine and seek to be seen.
- **career:** the work that lets you lead from center stage, need for recognition, vocational identity.
- **love:** wanting a partner who mirrors and admires your core self, risk of eclipsing the other.
- **money:** earning tied to self-worth and status, generosity when secure, spending to be seen.
- **family:** relationship to the father figure, the family's expectations of who you should become.
- **health:** core vitality and heart-centered energy, burnout when purpose is denied expression.
- **growth:** moving from performing an identity to authentically inhabiting one.

## Moon
- **Core archetype:** emotional needs, instinct, what makes you feel safe, the inner child.
- **career:** need for a nurturing, stable work environment, intuitive sensing of group mood.
- **love:** attachment style, need for emotional safety before intimacy, moodiness with partners.
- **money:** spending for comfort and security, hoarding or impulsive soothing purchases.
- **family:** relationship to the mother figure, early conditioning about safety and belonging.
- **health:** digestive and cyclical patterns, stress somatized through the gut, sleep and rhythm needs.
- **growth:** learning to self-soothe and meet your own needs instead of waiting to be rescued.

## Mercury
- **Core archetype:** mind, communication, learning style, how you connect ideas.
- **career:** negotiation, writing, teaching, the pace and style of processing information at work.
- **love:** talking things through as intimacy, need for mental rapport, how misunderstandings start.
- **money:** how you research and reason about purchases, talkative bargaining, scattered budgeting.
- **family:** sibling dynamics, the communication style modeled (or missing) in the household.
- **health:** nervous system load, anxiety from overthinking, restlessness, respiratory sensitivity.
- **growth:** slowing the mind enough to listen, trusting a thought before broadcasting it.

## Venus
- **Core archetype:** values, attraction, pleasure, how you relate and what you find beautiful.
- **career:** work in aesthetics, diplomacy, or relationship-building; reluctance toward harsh competition.
- **love:** what draws you in, capacity for give-and-take, conflict avoidance to preserve harmony.
- **money:** taste for comfort and quality, spending on beauty/connection, self-worth tied to having enough.
- **family:** the affection style modeled at home, peacekeeping role among relatives.
- **health:** kidney/skin sensitivity, weight tied to emotional comfort-seeking, love of sensory pleasure.
- **growth:** valuing yourself independent of being valued by others; love without self-erasure.

## Jupiter
- **Core archetype:** growth, meaning, optimism, where you expand and seek opportunity.
- **career:** appetite for risk-taking, teaching/mentoring roles, overcommitment from over-optimism.
- **love:** generosity and adventurousness with partners, restlessness if a relationship feels small.
- **money:** belief in abundance, overspending or overextending credit, luck mixed with excess.
- **family:** the family's philosophy/faith inheritance, the relative who encouraged your horizons.
- **health:** liver and metabolic themes, weight gain from excess, vitality from movement and travel.
- **growth:** turning faith into follow-through instead of escapism or grandiosity.

## Saturn
- **Core archetype:** structure, discipline, fear/mastery, where you mature through limitation.
- **career:** earned authority through persistence, fear of failure, late-blooming mastery.
- **love:** commitment-readiness, fear of inadequacy, relationships that demand real accountability.
- **money:** caution, long-term saving, scarcity anxiety even when resourced, respect for contracts.
- **family:** the disciplinarian or absent-parent theme, duty and obligation within the household.
- **health:** bones/joints/teeth, chronic conditions from suppressed stress, resilience built slowly.
- **growth:** transforming fear-based restriction into self-respecting structure and earned confidence.

## Uranus
- **Core archetype:** individuation, disruption, freedom, where you break from convention.
- **career:** innovation, sudden shifts, discomfort with hierarchy, needing autonomy to do good work.
- **love:** need for space within closeness, attraction to the unconventional, fear of being caged.
- **money:** erratic income or sudden windfalls/losses, investment in unconventional ventures.
- **family:** the rebel or outsider role, a household disruption that forced early independence.
- **health:** nervous system shocks, irregular patterns, healing through unconventional methods.
- **growth:** channeling rebellion into authentic individuation rather than reflexive disruption.

## Neptune
- **Core archetype:** imagination, transcendence, idealism/illusion, where boundaries dissolve.
- **career:** creative/healing/spiritual vocations, vulnerability to burnout or workplace illusions.
- **love:** romantic idealization, soulmate longing, risk of merging until the self disappears.
- **money:** vagueness about finances, susceptibility to scams or impractical investments.
- **family:** a parent experienced as absent, idealized, or addicted; unspoken family fog.
- **health:** unclear diagnoses, sensitivity to substances, healing through rest and the imaginal.
- **growth:** grounding inspiration into form instead of escapism, dissolving ego without losing self.

## Pluto
- **Core archetype:** power, depth, death/rebirth, where you transform through intensity.
- **career:** drive for control and depth, talent for crisis work, power struggles with authority.
- **love:** intense bonding, jealousy/control themes, transformation through emotional exposure.
- **money:** power dynamics around shared resources, debt or inheritance, obsessive control of finances.
- **family:** inherited secrets or trauma, a parent's hidden power struggles shaping the home.
- **health:** chronic or hidden conditions, regenerative crises, healing that requires facing the shadow.
- **growth:** surrendering control to allow genuine transformation rather than domination or collapse.

## North Node
- **Core archetype:** growth direction, the unfamiliar skill the life calls you toward.
- **career:** the stretch role that feels unearned at first but builds real mastery over time.
- **love:** the relational capacity (independence or merging) you must practice, not default to.
- **money:** the unfamiliar relationship to resources (self-reliance or interdependence) to develop.
- **family:** moving beyond inherited family roles toward a self-chosen way of belonging.
- **health:** building new habits that don't come naturally but serve long-term vitality.
- **growth:** consciously practicing the unfamiliar capacity rather than retreating to old comfort.

## Chiron
- **Core archetype:** the core wound and the healing gift that grows from tending it.
- **career:** the wound that becomes a vocation, mentoring others through what once disabled you.
- **love:** the old hurt that gets triggered in intimacy, becoming the doorway to real tenderness.
- **money:** feelings of lack or unworthiness around resources, healed by owning your value.
- **family:** the inherited wound passed through generations, met instead of merely repeated.
- **health:** the chronic or recurring issue that teaches rather than simply afflicts.
- **growth:** turning the wound into wisdom by tending it instead of hiding or being ruled by it.
