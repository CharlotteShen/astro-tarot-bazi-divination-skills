# Reading Method

Two modes, both grounded in the birth-data file, both obeying the guardrails below. The difference
is altitude: whole chart vs. one body.

## The cookbook failure (what this method exists to prevent)
Reading each placement in isolation ("Sun in Pisces means…, Moon in Cancer means…") and emitting
disconnected paragraphs. That reads like a listicle and says nothing specific. Every claim must
weave at least two chart factors.

## Mode A — whole-chart synthesis (default)

1. **Skeleton.** State the Big Three (Sun sign, Moon sign, Ascendant). Find the chart ruler (the
   planet ruling the Ascendant sign) and its sign/house/condition. Note element/modality balance,
   any stellium (3+ planets in one sign or house), and the tightest aspects.
2. **Signal triage.** Rank the 3–5 LOUDEST features: planets on angles (conj ASC/MC ≤6°), major
   aspects ≤3° orb, the chart ruler's condition, stelliums, and repeated themes (e.g. several
   Saturn contacts). These get the most words.
3. **Core story.** In plain language, name the chart's central tension(s) and central gift(s) —
   its spine. One or two sentences a friend would remember.
4. **Domain synthesis.** Cover: identity/vitality · inner/emotional · mind/communication ·
   relating · vocation/path · growth/shadow (Nodes, Chiron, Saturn, Pluto). For each domain weave
   the relevant placements + houses + aspects together. Lead with meaning; cite the factors after.
5. **Integration.** Tie it together as a developmental arc — how the tensions and gifts evolve.

## Mode B — per-planet drill-down (each star, in context, by scenario)

Trigger: "tell me about my Mars", "what does my Venus mean for money", "my Saturn at work".

1. **Archetype** — the planet's core function in one or two lines (from `planets.md`).
2. **In your chart** — localize it to its sign, house, aspects, and condition. This is the bulk.
3. **Through the scenario** — read it for the requested lens. Scenarios are BOTH:
   - fixed life-domain lenses: career, love, money, family, health, growth (use `planets.md`'s
     by-domain hooks);
   - any open situation the user describes in the moment.
4. **Honest two-sided take** — the gift AND the friction of this placement for that scenario.

**Drill-down guardrail:** weave the planet's sign + house + aspects for the specific scenario. Never
a generic "Mars in Aries means…" line. The same planet must read differently across charts and
across scenarios.

## Enrichment layer (optional)
On a drill-down, or when a derived point is notably tight, bring in the `enrichment` data
(see `references/enrichment.md`): the Lots (Fortune/Spirit/Eros), the Sun/Moon and occupied midpoints,
declination parallels, and the Vertex. Treat them as a supporting layer — don't dump them into a default
whole-chart reading. Flag the time-dependent ones (lots, vertex, houses) if the birth time is unknown.

## Q&A mode
For follow-up questions, read the birth-data file and answer chart-specifically and two-sidedly
(strengths and challenges), always tracing back to named factors. Never fatalistic.

## BaZi bridge (optional — only if birth data exists AND the querent opts in)
If `natal-astrology/birth-data.md` has the fields BaZi needs (date/time/place + `bazi_gender`), you
may OFFER once, at the end, to add a 八字 (Chinese Four Pillars) resonance layer. On opt-in, follow
`bazi/references/natal-bridge.md` (repo-root relative) — at most 1–2 thematic-echo notes (or one meaningful divergence),
each flagged "a thematic echo, not a translation" (五行 ≠ the four Western elements; 十神 ≠ planets).
Supporting color only; never required, never steering. Convergence deepens a theme, never the fate.
Pure natal stays pure.

## Hard guardrails (both modes)
- Never list placements in isolation; synthesize ≥2 factors per claim where possible.
- Adjudicate contradictions — do not average into "a bit of both". Pick the dominant signal and say
  why, then note the counter-current.
- No Barnum/vague statements ("you can be both outgoing and shy"). Be specific.
- ~70% plain language / ~30% technical. Lead with meaning; data supports.
- Empower, don't predict. Free-will framing.
- Name hard placements honestly but developmentally — no doom, no medical/death claims.
- NEVER reference a position or aspect that is not in the birth-data file.
